Nash Equilibrium: No incentive to change

A Nash Equilibrium is a stable state in a game or interactive situation where no player has an incentive to unilaterally change their chosen strategy.

Today's takeaway

Individual rational decisions can lead to sub-optimal stable outcomes; to change a system, we must change the game's rules or payoffs.

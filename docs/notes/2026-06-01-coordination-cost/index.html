Coordination cost grows when more people must stay aligned

Adding people to a shared effort can increase the number of relationships, decisions, and handoffs that need attention.

Takeaway

Before adding capacity to a system, check whether coordination will become the new bottleneck.

What I learned

Coordination cost is the effort required to keep people, tasks, and decisions aligned. As a group grows, communication paths multiply faster than the number of people.

Why it matters

More people can increase capacity, but they also create more meetings, handoffs, updates, and shared assumptions to manage. A team can become slower even while adding talent if the coordination structure does not improve.

A simple example

Two people need one relationship to stay aligned. Five people can have ten pairwise relationships. The work may still be simple, but keeping everyone synchronized becomes a separate job.

How I can use it

  • Ask whether a delay is caused by too little labor or too much coordination.
  • Clarify ownership before adding more people.
  • Reduce unnecessary handoffs.
  • Use shared artifacts so people do not need constant meetings to stay aligned.

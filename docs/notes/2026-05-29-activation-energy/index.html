Activation energy is the push needed to start change

Many changes require an initial input of energy before they can continue, even when the final state is more stable.

Takeaway

To make a change easier, lower the starting barrier instead of only wanting the final outcome more.

What I learned

Activation energy is the minimum energy needed for a reaction or process to begin. A change can be favorable overall and still need an initial push before it gets moving.

Why it matters

This separates the value of a destination from the difficulty of starting. A system may stay where it is because the first step is costly, confusing, or uncomfortable. Lowering that first barrier can matter more than making the final reward larger.

A simple example

Paper can burn and release heat, but it does not burst into flame at room temperature. A spark supplies enough initial energy to start the reaction.

How I can use it

  • Identify the first barrier, not only the final goal.
  • Make the starting step smaller and more obvious.
  • Use reminders, defaults, or setup work as catalysts.
  • Remember that stuck systems may need a trigger, not a new destination.
